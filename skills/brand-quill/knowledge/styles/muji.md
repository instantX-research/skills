# 无印良品 MUJI — Style Knowledge Base

## Aesthetic Position
Restraint, white space, return to essence. No decoration, no exaggeration, no provocation. Like an "empty vessel," letting the reader fill in their own feelings.

## Founders & Core Creators
- **田中一光** Ikko Tanaka — First-generation art director, established the visual language
- **小池一子** Kazuko Koike — First-generation copywriter, wrote「わけあって、安い。」
- **原研哉** Kenya Hara — Art director from 2002, "Horizon" and "Emptiness" series

## Writing Principles

### Do
- Convey the most meaning with the fewest words
- Start from "nothingness," "emptiness," "nature"
- Let the reader complete the imagination within the white space
- Use declarative sentences; tone as calm as water
- Focus on materials, processes, and the everyday itself
- **State facts first (materials/processes), let the reader draw their own emotional conclusions** — this is MUJI's most essential rhetorical technique
- Make good use of philosophical questioning (「気持ちいいのはなぜだろう。」)

### Don't
- No exclamation marks
- No superlatives like "best," "top-tier," "ultimate"
- No piling up of adjectives
- No emotional provocation
- No manufacturing anxiety or desire
- Never mention "brand" — MUJI itself means "no-brand"

## Sentence Templates

```
[Natural imagery] is the [essence] of [everyday thing].
[Four-character phrase]. (white space)
Not [excessive], but [just right].
[Material/process], [verb] [human feeling]. (fact → emotion)
[One or two characters]. (minimalist title: 「家。」「廿年。」)
[......]のはなぜだろう。(philosophical questioning)
Goodbye, at [scene A], [scene B], [scene C]...... see you. (enumeration + white space)
```

## Copy Density
- Headline: 2-8 characters
- Body: 1-3 sentences, each no more than 20 characters
- Total word count: keep under 50 characters
