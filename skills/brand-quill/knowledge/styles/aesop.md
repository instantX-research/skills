# Aesop — Style Knowledge Base

## Aesthetic Position
Literary, sensory, intellectually restrained. Like a book of poetry left beside the bathroom sink — never selling, only inviting you to slow down and feel.

## Founder
- **Dennis Paphitis** — "A product needs to perform, but if it can do so with a little poetry, so much the better."

## Writing Principles

### Do
- Use literary language to describe sensory experiences (touch, scent, texture)
- Quote from literature, philosophy, and poetry
- Use botanical names rather than common names
- Every word must be precise and carry weight
- Maintain an unhurried, composed tone
- Describe the origin and characteristics of ingredients

### Don't
- Never use 「天然」 or 「有机」 ("natural" or "organic" — explicitly prohibited by brand policy)
- Never use superlatives (「最好的」「最有效的」 / "the best," "the most effective")
- Never make efficacy promises (don't say "look 10 years younger")
- Never use exclamation marks
- Never chase trendy slang
- Never use emoji or internet speak
- Never use gendered language (all product descriptions must be gender-neutral)
- Never address the reader directly as "you/your" (maintain an observer's distance)

### The Art of Product Naming
Aesop's product names are copy in themselves:
- **Resurrection** — the hand wash isn't called "hand wash"; it's called "resurrection"
- **Reverence** — reverence, devotion
- **Parsley Seed** — botanical name used directly in the product name
- **In Two Minds** — named for combination skin, evoking indecision
- **Fabulous Face Oil** — straightforward praise that becomes elegant through contrast
- **Lucent** — luminous, clear
- **Marvelous** — outstanding
- **Hwyl** — a Welsh word meaning "an exhilarating feeling"
- **Tacit** — understood without being spoken
- **Rozu** — a transliteration of the Japanese word for "rose"

## Sentence Templates

```
A [adjective], [adjective] formulation [functional description], with [botanical ingredient] and [botanical ingredient].
[Sensory verb] the [body part] with [ingredient origin] [botanical name].
[Literary quotation] — [Author name] (store quotation format)
```

## Copy Density
- Product descriptions: 1-2 sentences, 15-25 words each
- Ingredient lists use Latin botanical names
- Store quotations: a single sentence from a literary or philosophical luminary
- Overall tone: like reading the sidebar of a design magazine
