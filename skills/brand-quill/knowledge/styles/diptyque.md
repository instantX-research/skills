# Diptyque — Style Knowledge Base

## Aesthetic Position
Parisian sensibility, sensory narrative, memory travel. Every piece of copy is like opening a bottle of perfume — first the top note as imagery, then the heart note as story, and finally the base note as emotion.

## Brand Background
Founded in 1961 at 34 boulevard Saint-Germain, Paris. Three founders: Desmond Knox-Leet (painter), Christiane Montadre-Gautrot (textile/interior designer), Yves Coueslant (theater director). Their artistic DNA defines the literary quality of the brand's copy.

## Writing Principles

### Do
- Use sensory language: scent, texture, temperature, light
- Paint scenes and memories, not features
- Use specific place names, plant names, seasons
- Speak as if softly recounting a travel memory
- Maintain French elegance: don't over-explain, leave room for imagination
- Mix in French vocabulary for added texture
- Use **synesthesia** (describe scent through touch, describe color through temperature)
- Use **seasonal/temporal anchoring** ("winter arrives," "the mimosas have finally arrived")

### Don't
- Don't bluntly say "this candle smells like XX"
- Don't use "premium," "luxury," "high-end" (the brand already is)
- Don't rush — narrative pacing should be slow
- Don't use scientific or chemical terminology
- Don't promote around price
- Don't use first-person singular — the brand voice uses "we/nous" for the Maison, or an impersonal observational perspective

## Classic Copy — Français Original

1. "34 boulevard Saint-Germain." — The brand address as identity
2. "L'art du soin par les plantes." — The art of plant-based care
3. "Un voyage olfactif." — An olfactory journey
4. "Chaque bougie raconte une histoire." — Each candle tells a story
5. "Baies: un bouquet de roses et de feuilles de cassis." — Berries: a bouquet of roses and blackcurrant leaves
6. "Figuier: la chaleur verte d'un figuier méditerranéen." — Fig Tree: the green warmth of a Mediterranean fig tree
7. "Eau Duelle: la vanille, épicée et boisée, entre ombre et lumière." — Eau Duelle: vanilla, spicy and woody, between shadow and light

## Classic Copy — English Original

### Classic Candle Descriptions (official copy)
1. "Baies evokes the scent of roses warmed by blackcurrant leaves." — Baies (diptyque's best-selling product)
2. "Philosykos captures the warmth and verdant stillness of a Greek fig grove." — Greek fig
3. "This sophisticated blend of rare woods recalls the crackling of an open fire. Warm and familiar during the winter days." — Feu de Bois (firewood)

5. "On the Tanneron hills near Grasse, the mimosas have finally arrived. Their small, downy blossoms release velvety, lightly honeyed notes." — Mimosa

## Sentence Templates

```
[Place name]の[season/time], [plant]が[sensory verb]. [Texture/temperature]の[noun]が[emotion]を呼び覚ます.
A [sensory adjective] [noun] of [origin] [plant], [verb]ing [memory/scene].
At [place] during [moment], the [scent/texture] of [natural element], [verb]ing like [metaphor].
```

## Copy Density
- Product name: French original, never translated
- Description: 1-2 sentences, 15-30 words in English
- Tone: whispered, reminiscent, travel, sensory
- Pacing: slow, like the burn rate of a candle
