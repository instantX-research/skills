# Guinness — Style Knowledge Base

## Aesthetic Position
The poetry of patience, the romance of waiting, the strength in substance. Guinness copy is built on a single insight: good things take time. The 119.5 seconds it takes to pour a perfect pint becomes a philosophy for life. The brand voice is warm, wise, Irish-inflected, and deeply human.

## Founders & Core Creators
- **Arthur Guinness** — Founded 1759 in Dublin, Ireland
- **John Gilroy** — Illustrator behind the iconic 1930s-50s poster campaigns ("My Goodness, My Guinness!")
- **AMV BBDO** — Agency partner from 1998, created the legendary "Surfer" ad (1999, directed by Jonathan Glazer), often voted the greatest TV commercial ever made

## Writing Principles

### Do
- Turn waiting into a virtue — patience is the core brand metaphor
- Use warm, human storytelling about character and resilience
- Write with an Irish sense of rhythm — poetic but never pretentious
- Celebrate substance over flash (dark beer, not light; depth, not speed)
- Use contrast: power and gentleness, darkness and light, waiting and reward
- Let the pour (119.5 seconds) be a metaphor for anything worth having

### Don't
- Never rush — the pacing should feel like watching a Guinness settle
- Never be cynical or ironic — Guinness is earnest
- Never position the product as trendy or fashionable
- Never be lightweight — every word should have weight and substance
- Never use flashy language — prefer dark, warm, rich words
- Never explain the metaphor — trust the audience

## Classic Copy — English Original

### Historic
1. "Guinness is Good For You." — 1929, original tagline (now retired for health regulation reasons)
2. "My Goodness, My Guinness!" — 1935, John Gilroy poster campaign
3. "Guinness for Strength." — 1930s, Gilroy era
4. "Lovely Day for a Guinness." — 1930s-50s
5. "I've never tried it because I don't like it." — Classic poster, self-aware humor

### Modern Era (AMV BBDO)
6. "Good things come to those who wait." — 1996, the defining Guinness tagline
7. "Pure Genius." — 1980s-90s campaign
8. "Believe." — 2000s campaign
9. "Made of More." — 2013 brand platform
10. "Made of Black." — African market campaign celebrating Black identity

### Surfer Ad (1999, directed by Jonathan Glazer)
11. "He waits. That's what he does." — Opening narration
12. "Tick followed tock followed tick followed tock followed tick..." — Building tension
13. "And here it is. The reward." — The wave arrives

### Sapeurs Campaign (Made of More, 2014)
14. "The choices we make reveal the true nature of our character." — Opening line
15. "They call themselves the Sapeurs." — About Congo's Society of Elegant Persons

### Other Notable
16. "It's alive inside." — Surger campaign
17. "Even at the gates of darkness, good things await those who wait." — Print
18. "Out of darkness comes light." — Referencing the dark beer with white head
19. "There's more than meets the eye." — Double meaning: the beer and the person
20. "Guinness doesn't do fast." — Self-aware brand truth

## Classic Copy — Irish (Gaeilge)

1. "Ní neart go cur le chéile." — There is no strength without unity (used in rugby partnerships)

## Sentence Templates

```
Good things come to those who [verb].
[He/She/They] [wait/endure/persist]. That's what [he/she/they] [do/does].
Out of [darkness/hardship], [light/reward/beauty].
[Number] seconds. That's how long it takes to [mundane act]. And that's [enough/everything/the point].
Made of [unexpected quality].
```

## Copy Density
- Tagline: 5-10 words, warm and resonant
- Narration: Can go long — the Surfer ad builds slowly over 60 seconds
- Print: Short headline, minimal body — the image does the work
- Tone: A storyteller in a Dublin pub, after the second pint, when the stories get philosophical
- Rhythm: Slow build, slow build, slow build — then the reward. Like pouring a Guinness.
