# Patagonia — Style Knowledge Base

## Aesthetic Position
Honest, anti-consumerism, activism. The copy isn't selling something — it's urging you to buy less and care more about the planet. The brand is a stance.

## Founder
- **Yvon Chouinard** — "Earth is now our only shareholder."

## Writing Principles

### Do
- Tell the truth, even when it hurts sales
- Use specific numbers to show environmental costs
- Tell stories — about mountains, rivers, repairs, heritage
- Use first person "we," like a friend in conversation
- Acknowledge your own imperfections
- Encourage repair over buying new

### Don't
- Don't use flowery language
- Don't manufacture consumer desire
- Don't avoid controversial topics
- Don't use words like "luxury," "exclusive," or "limited edition"
- Don't romanticize outdoor activities to the point of dishonesty

## Classic Copy — English Original

### Brand-level
1. "Don't Buy This Jacket." — 2011, Black Friday, New York Times full-page ad
2. "We're in business to save our home planet." — Brand mission
3. "Earth is now our only shareholder." — 2022, Yvon Chouinard gave away the company
4. "1% for the Planet." — Since 1985, donating 1% of revenue to environmental organizations; formally established as a named initiative in 2002
5. "Build the best product, cause no unnecessary harm, use business to inspire and implement solutions to the environmental crisis." — Mission statement
6. "The President Stole Your Land." — 2017, opposing the reduction of national monuments
7. "Vote the Assholes Out." — 2020, hidden copy on pants label
8. "Buy Less, Demand More." — Brand tagline

### Don't Buy This Jacket — Body Text Excerpt
9. "To make this jacket required 135 liters of water, enough to meet the daily needs of 45 people."
10. "Its journey from its origin as 60% recycled polyester to our Reno warehouse generated nearly 20 pounds of carbon dioxide."
11. "Don't buy what you don't need. Think twice before you buy anything."
12. "Ask yourself: Do I need this? Is it built to last? Can I repair it?"

### Worn Wear Series (Repair Culture)
13. "Worn Wear: Better Than New." — Secondhand repair program
14. "The stories we wear." — Worn Wear subtitle
15. "If it's broke, fix it." — Repair philosophy
16. "One of the most responsible things we can do as a company is to make high-quality stuff that lasts for years."

### Product Copy Style
17. "This is our most waterproof, most breathable, most mechanically tough jacket." — Torrentshell (direct, functional)
18. "Fair Trade Certified sewn." — Labeled on every product
19. "Made with 100% recycled polyester." — Material transparency

### Environmental Action
20. "The cleanest product is one that already exists." — Anti-consumerism manifesto
21. "We guarantee everything we make. If you are not satisfied, return it for a repair, replacement, or refund." — Ironclad Guarantee
22. "Reduce what you buy. Repair what you can. Reuse/recycle everything else."

### Yvon Chouinard Classic Quotes (The founder's words are the brand's copy)
23. "The more you know, the less you need." — Yvon Chouinard
24. "Going back to a simpler life is not a step backward."
25. "How you climb a mountain is more important than reaching the top."
26. "I've been a businessman for almost sixty years. It's as difficult for me to say those words as it is for someone to admit being an alcoholic."
27. "Let My People Go Surfing." — Chouinard's autobiography title, also synonymous with the company's flexible work culture

### Don't Buy This Jacket — Full Call to Action
28. "Because Patagonia wants to last in business for a long time — long enough to fix the environmental damage we've all done — we ask you to buy less and to reflect before you spend a dime on this jacket or anything else."
29. "It would be hypocritical for us to work for environmental change without encouraging customers to think before they buy."

## Sentence Templates

```
Don't [consumer behavior]. [Alternative behavior].
We [acknowledge the problem]. [Show the action taken].
[Specific number] of [environmental cost]. That's [everyday comparison].
[Product name]. [Function]. [Eco-friendly material]. [Call to action].
```

## Copy Density
- Headlines: 3-7 words, direct, high-impact
- Body text: Can be long — Patagonia isn't afraid of long copy, but every sentence must carry information
- Tone: Like a letter from a friend you trust
