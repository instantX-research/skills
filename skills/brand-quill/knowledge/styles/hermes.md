# Hermès — Style Knowledge Base

## Aesthetic Position
Poetic restraint, timeless craft, French nonchalance. Hermès copy never sells — it strolls. Like a well-dressed flaneur who happens to notice something beautiful and mentions it in passing. The luxury is assumed, never stated.

## Brand Background
- Founded 1837 in Paris as a harness workshop
- Six generations of family craftsmanship
- Unlike other luxury houses, Hermès never uses celebrity endorsements and rarely mentions price
- Every campaign is built around a seasonal theme expressed through illustration, photography, and poetic copy

## Writing Principles

### Do
- Frame everything through the lens of craft, time, and heritage
- Use poetic imagery — Hermès copy reads like verse
- Maintain an air of elegant nonchalance — never try too hard
- Use seasonal and nature-based metaphors
- Let the object speak through its making, not its price
- Employ wordplay and double meanings with French sophistication
- Treat each campaign as a chapter in an ongoing story

### Don't
- Never mention price, exclusivity, or "luxury"
- Never use urgency ("limited," "last chance," "exclusive offer")
- Never explain the craft in technical terms — evoke it
- Never be flashy or loud
- Never use celebrity names as endorsements
- Never compare to competitors — Hermès exists in its own universe

## Classic Copy — French Original

1. "Hermès, artisan contemporain depuis 1837." — Contemporary artisan since 1837
2. "Le Flâneur d'Hermès." — The Hermès Flaneur (campaign theme)
3. "La vie comme un roman." — Life like a novel
4. "Les mains d'Hermès." — The hands of Hermès (craftsman campaign)
5. "Lignes de la main." — Lines of the hand (double meaning: palm lines / product lines)

## Classic Copy — English Original

### Campaign Themes
Each season gets a poetic title:

6. "The Gift of Time." — 2014 holiday, watches
7. "Flânerie." — The art of aimless wandering
8. "J'aime mon carré." — I love my scarf (participatory campaign)
9. "Hermès at Home." — Domestic life, elevated
10. "Objects carry stories. Stories carry time. Time carries everything." — Brand philosophy

### Product Copy Style

11. "A saddle-stitched companion for a lifetime of journeys." — Bag description style
12. "Silk that remembers the wind." — Scarf description style
13. "Time, told beautifully." — Watch
14. "Hands that have learned patience." — About Hermès artisans

### Brand Narrative

15. "Every object we create is an invitation to slow down."
16. "We don't make products. We make things that outlive us."
17. "The beauty of leather is that it remembers everything."
18. "At Hermès, there are no shortcuts. Only craft."

## Sentence Templates

- `[Object] that [poetic verb] [abstract noun].` — Silk that remembers the wind.
- `[Craft concept], since [year].`
- `[Abstract noun] carries [abstract noun]. [Abstract noun] carries everything.`
- `A [material]-[technique] [object] for a lifetime of [experience].`

## Copy Density

- **Tagline:** 3-8 words, reads like a line from a poem
- **Product description:** 1 sentence, never more — the object should feel self-evident
- **Campaign theme:** 2-5 words, seasonal and evocative
- **Tone:** A whispered aside at a gallery opening
- **Rhythm:** Measured, stately, like a horse's walk (a nod to equestrian heritage)
