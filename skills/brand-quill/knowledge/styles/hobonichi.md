# ほぼ日 Hobonichi — Style Knowledge Base

## Aesthetic Position
Gentle everyday philosophy. Hobonichi (Almost Daily) copy celebrates the unremarkable — a Tuesday afternoon, a cup of tea, the fact that you woke up. Founded by legendary copywriter 糸井重里 (Itoi Shigesato), who wrote some of Japan's most iconic taglines before creating Hobonichi as a platform for "finding the interesting in the ordinary."

## Founders & Core Creators
- **糸井重里** Itoi Shigesato — Founder, copywriter, creative director. One of Japan's most celebrated copywriters — he wrote taglines for Studio Ghibli, Seibu/Parco, Nissan, and more
- Founded 1998 as an online magazine「ほぼ日刊イトイ新聞」(Almost Daily Itoi Newspaper). Now also known for the Hobonichi Techo (planner) and lifestyle goods

## Writing Principles

### Do
- Celebrate the ordinary and unremarkable
- Use childlike simplicity — as if explaining life to a curious five-year-old
- Be warm without being sweet — sincere, never saccharine
- Use conversational tone, as if writing to a friend
- Find profundity in trivial observations
- End with a gentle surprise or a quiet turn

### Don't
- Never be pretentious or overly literary (unlike Aesop)
- Never use difficult words when simple ones will do
- Never moralize or lecture
- Never be sarcastic or edgy
- Never rush — every sentence should feel like a slow exhale
- Never manufacture profundity — find it naturally

## Classic Copy — Original Chinese

Hobonichi has limited Chinese market presence, but the philosophy translates.

1. 「温柔地，坚强地，有趣地。」— Hobonichi motto, Chinese interpretation
2. 「什么都没发生的日子，恭喜你。」

## Original English Copy

1. "Gently, strongly, interestingly." — Hobonichi motto
2. "The BOOK of LIFE." — Hobonichi Techo tagline
3. "Live." — Princess Mononoke (1997)

## Sentence Templates

```
[Mundane observation]. [Gentle philosophical turn].
[Simple verb]。(Live. Eat. Sleep. Play.)
[Thing everyone knows], [said as if discovering it for the first time].
Congratulations on [something unremarkable].
[Conversational question], [warm answer].
```

## Copy Density
- Tagline: 3-10 characters, could be spoken by a child
- Body: conversational length, like a diary entry — 1-5 sentences
- Tone: your wisest, kindest friend who happens to notice everything
- Rhythm: Unhurried, like a Sunday morning with nowhere to go
