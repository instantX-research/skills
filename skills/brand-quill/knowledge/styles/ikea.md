# IKEA — Style Knowledge Base

## Aesthetic Position
Democratic, warm, everyday poetry. The copy reads like a life-savvy friend chatting with you — humorous, grounded, turning ordinary home life into small moments of happiness.

## Brand Background
Founded in 1943 by Ingvar Kamprad in Sweden. Core philosophy: "Creating a better everyday life for the many people" (Democratic Design). Five dimensions: form, function, quality, sustainability, and low price.

## Writing Principles

### Do
- Tell stories through everyday scenes (waking up, cooking, children playing)
- Add a touch of humor and playfulness
- Use "you" for direct conversation, like a friend
- Give furniture its own personality and character
- Make readers want to go home after reading the copy
- Acknowledge life's messiness, then embrace it

### Don't
- Don't be condescending
- Don't use words like "luxury," "premium," or "top-tier"
- Don't overcomplicate — IKEA copy should be understandable even by a child
- Don't shy away from life's reality (a chaotic morning is still a good morning)
- Don't just describe product features — describe the feeling it brings to life

## Sentence Templates

```
[Everyday scene], [unexpected small happiness].
[Furniture/object], like [personified metaphor], [verb].
[Everyday problem]? There's a [IKEA solution] for that.
[Short phrase]. [Short phrase]. And that's enough.
```

## Copy Density
- Headline: 5-10 words, conversational tone
- Product description: 1-3 sentences, vivid and visual
- Brand narrative: warm, down-to-earth, feels like a chat
- Humor level: moderate — a knowing smile, not a belly laugh
