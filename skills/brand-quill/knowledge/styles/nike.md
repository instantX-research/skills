# Nike — Style Knowledge Base

## Aesthetic Position
Action, empowerment, provocation. Nike copy doesn't describe — it pushes. Every sentence is a starting gun. The voice says: stop thinking, start moving. You are already an athlete; you just need to believe it.

## Founders & Core Creators
- **Phil Knight** & **Bill Bowerman** — Co-founders, 1964 (as Blue Ribbon Sports), renamed Nike in 1971
- **Dan Wieden** (Wieden+Kennedy) — Created "Just Do It" in 1988, inspired by death row inmate Gary Gilmore's last words "Let's do it"

## Writing Principles

### Do
- Use imperative sentences — commands, not suggestions
- Address the reader directly as "you"
- Use strong action verbs (run, dream, fight, rise, play)
- Make the ordinary person feel like an athlete
- Take a stand — Nike copy is never neutral on social issues
- Use short, punchy rhythm — every word hits like a heartbeat
- Turn obstacles into fuel ("They said... So I...")

### Don't
- Never be passive or contemplative
- Never use soft, hedging language ("maybe," "try to," "a little")
- Never describe the product specs — describe what the person becomes
- Never be safe or forgettable — Nike would rather be controversial than boring
- Never use exclamation marks (the confidence is in the words, not the punctuation)
- Never explain the joke or the metaphor — trust the reader

### Dream Crazy — Full Narration (2018, voiced by Colin Kaepernick)

> If people say your dreams are crazy, if they laugh at what you think you can do, good. Stay that way. Because what non-believers fail to understand is that calling a dream crazy is not an insult. It's a compliment. Don't try to be the fastest runner in your school or the fastest in the world. Be the fastest ever. Don't picture yourself wearing OBJ's jersey. Picture OBJ wearing yours. Don't settle for homecoming queen or linebacker. Do both. Lose 120 pounds and become an Ironman after beating a brain tumor. Don't believe you have to be like anybody to be somebody. If you're born a refugee, don't let it stop you from playing soccer for the national team. At age 16, become the youngest pro in the history of tennis. If you have only one hand, don't just watch football. Play it at the highest level. And if you're a girl from Compton, don't just dream of playing tennis. Dream of changing it. Yeah, that's more like it. So don't ask if your dreams are crazy. Ask if they're crazy enough.

## Sentence Templates

```
Just [verb] it.
Don't [limitation]. [Bigger aspiration].
If they say [obstacle], show them [achievement].
[One-word imperative]. (冲。跑。炼。)
[Verb]出[result]. (踢出传奇。活出伟大。打出名堂。)
You can't stop [what we do together].
It's not about [what you think]. It's about [what you do].
```

## Copy Density
- Tagline: 1-5 words, punch like a fist
- Manifesto: Can go long (Dream Crazy = 200+ words), but every sentence must land
- Chinese: Often a single action verb as the entire copy (「冲。」「炼。」)
- Rhythm: Staccato. Short sentences. No padding. Every word earns its place.
- Tone: A coach who believes in you more than you believe in yourself
